# Cloud vs. On-Premise CMMS: What Actually Matters for a Multi-Site Portfolio

For a single building, the choice between cloud and on-premise facility management software is mostly a matter of preference. For an organization managing multiple sites — a manufacturing group with several plants, a hospital network, an education campus with multiple blocks — it's an operational decision with consequences that last years.

## Why the calculation changes at multi-site scale

On-premise systems put the infrastructure burden on your own IT team: servers, licensing, backups, and security patching, replicated at every location. Adding a second site doesn't just mean rolling out software — it often means a standalone infrastructure project with its own timeline and budget.

Cloud CMMS shifts that burden to the vendor. A new site gets added through an admin panel rather than a procurement cycle. For organizations planning to grow their footprint, this difference compounds every time a new facility comes online.

## Where each approach genuinely wins

**On-premise makes sense when:**
- Data sovereignty or regulatory requirements mandate that maintenance data physically stay on local infrastructure
- The organization already has dedicated IT resources to own patching, backup, and uptime
- Network connectivity between sites and any central system is unreliable or heavily restricted

**Cloud makes sense when:**
- Facility teams need mobile access without routing through a VPN — which matters enormously for field technician adoption
- The organization is actively adding sites and doesn't want infrastructure to be the bottleneck on growth
- IT bandwidth is limited, and offloading hosting, patching, and backup to a vendor is a net gain
- Leadership wants portfolio-level visibility — comparing maintenance cost and asset health across all sites from a single dashboard, rather than stitching together separate reports from separate systems

For most growing facility portfolios, cloud has become the practical default — not because on-premise is obsolete, but because the operational overhead of scaling it across multiple sites rarely pays for itself outside of genuinely regulated, security-sensitive environments.

## The question multi-site teams underestimate: portfolio visibility

The infrastructure debate gets most of the attention, but the bigger operational shift for multi-site organizations is what a unified system enables: comparing sites against each other. When maintenance cost, asset health, and compliance status live in one system across every location, underperforming sites become visible months before their costs show up as a surprise in a financial report. Managed as isolated buildings with separate systems, that same pattern stays hidden until it's expensive.

## A practical way to decide

Rather than starting from "cloud vs. on-premise" as an ideological question, it's worth starting from three concrete answers specific to your organization:

1. Does any site face a regulatory requirement that data cannot leave local infrastructure?
2. How much unplanned IT overhead can your team realistically absorb per new site added?
3. Do your technicians need reliable mobile access in the field, and is your current network setup (VPN-dependent or not) going to support that well?

Those three answers, more than any general industry trend, will tell you which model actually fits your portfolio — and save you from a decision made on infrastructure philosophy rather than operational reality.
